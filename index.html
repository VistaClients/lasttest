International Yoga Day

  • 5:00 AM - 9:00 PM
  • Inter Continental Garden, Mumbai.

Yoga day is celebrated on 21st June every year. This year a special session was organized for the students of the Primary Section.
The session was conducted by the instructor, Chantelle Cabral. Children learnt a few asanas, such as Tadasana (the mountain pose), Vrikshasana (the tree pose) and Shavasana, to name a few. Students were also introduced to breathing techniques, i.e. pranayamas for awareness and relaxation.
The half-hour session helped the students to understand many benefits of yoga.

Std 12th Farewell

  • 5:00 AM - 9:00 PM
  • Inter Continental Garden, Mumbai.

The morning of 3rd February was filled with mixed emotions, as students and teachers of St. Vincent’s came together to bid farewell to the outgoing students of Std. 12th.
The day began with a special morning assembly, conducted by the students of Std. 11th. The evening farewell program began with the auspicious lighting of the lamp by the students, signifying that they are the light of the world. After a short prayer service, Rev. Fr. Patekar addressed the outgoing students of Std.12th with an inspiring speech.
Lastly, the 11th graders gave a small, warm-hearted, and soulful message to their very dear seniors followed by the stunning performance by the live band.
The dance floor was open for the students as they danced their hearts out to the tapping beats of the DJ. The students clicked many pictures trying to capture their memories with their friends and teachers. Finally, there was a buffet dinner arranged and with that, the beautiful evening came to an end.

Interclass English Debate Competition - Std. 9 and 10

  • 5:00 AM - 9:00 PM
  • Inter Continental Garden, Mumbai.

“You don't win a debate by suppressing discussion; you win it with a better argument.” The Interclass English Debate Competition for Std. IX and X was held on Tuesday, the 29th of November 2022 in the Gulati Hall. The event began at 9 a.m. straight after the school assembly.
The topic for the debate was ‘Are Offline Classes Better Than Online Ones?’ Each student was well prepared, and they presented their argument very effectively. The students put across very valid points to support their point of view. The debate gave a clear insight into the pros and cons of offline and online teaching. It was truly an enlightening experience. The judges for the competition were two highly experienced teachers from our own school. The competition ended at 10 a.m.
